Patna Sahib: Birthplace of Guru Gobind Singh Ji

Explore the sacred Takht Sri Harmandir Sahib in the heart of Bihar’s capital.

Patna Sahib, also known as Takht Sri Harmandir Sahib, is one of the five Takhts (seats of authority) of Sikhism and holds immense spiritual significance. Located in Patna, the capital of Bihar, it is the birthplace of the tenth Sikh Guru, Guru Gobind Singh Ji, who was born here in 1666. This holy shrine is a place of pilgrimage not only for Sikhs but also for people of all faiths who come to witness its peace and grandeur.

The gurdwara was built by Maharaja Ranjit Singh in the 19th century to honor the Guru. The white marble structure, adorned with golden domes and intricate architecture, stands as a symbol of devotion and history. Inside, you’ll find sacred relics belonging to Guru Gobind Singh Ji, including weapons, a cradle, and handwritten scriptures.

The complex houses a langar (community kitchen) that serves free food to thousands of devotees daily. The spiritual atmosphere, combined with the serene kirtan (devotional music), creates a sense of divine peace. Visitors often describe the experience as soul-soothing and uplifting.

Patna Sahib is especially vibrant during Gurpurab, the birth anniversary of Guru Gobind Singh Ji. Devotees from all over the world gather to pay homage, participate in processions, and engage in community service. The gurdwara is a powerful reminder of the values of equality, courage, and compassion preached by the Guru.

If you are in Bihar, Patna Sahib is a must-visit destination that not only showcases Sikh heritage but also reflects the inclusive spiritual legacy of the region. Its historical and cultural significance makes it one of the most respected religious landmarks in India.
